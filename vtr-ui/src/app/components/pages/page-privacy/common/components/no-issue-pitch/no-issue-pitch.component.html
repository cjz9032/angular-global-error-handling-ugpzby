Did you know?

About 4.5 billion records with private information were exposed as a result of data breaches in 2018. Among companies hit by breaches are Under Armour (150 million records), United States Postal Service (60 million records), Quora (100 million records), Marriott International (500 million records), British Airways (380 million records), MyHeritage (92 million records) and many others.

Most websites collect this information about you:

IP Address
Location
Social Profile Information
Shopping History
Device Information
Online Behaviour Patterns
Websites Visited

This data is used to personalize your experience, show targeted ads, or suggest things based on your interests.

If you store passwords for your bank accounts or social media profiles in your web browser, you risk sharing this private information unintentionally with apps and extensions on your PC. People often reuse the same password for many websites. This leads to multiple account breaches if the password exposed.
